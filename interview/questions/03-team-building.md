# Team Building and Org Design

This round assesses your ability to build, scale, and structure engineering teams. Companies test whether you can hire effectively, onboard efficiently, and design team structures that set people up for success.


## Format

Typically 45-60 minutes. The interviewer explores your approach to hiring, team composition, organizational design, and scaling challenges. Some companies combine this with people management; others have a dedicated round.


## Questions

### Hiring

- Walk me through how you design an interview loop for a senior engineer. What rounds would you include and why?
- How do you calibrate interviewers to ensure consistency across your hiring panel?
- You need to hire 4 engineers in the next quarter. How do you prioritize - experience level, skill set, diversity?
- Describe a time you made a bad hire. What happened and what did you learn?
- How do you close a competitive candidate who has multiple offers?
- What signals do you look for in a phone screen that indicate a strong culture fit?
- How do you balance hiring for current needs versus future team composition?
- How do you partner with recruiting to source diverse candidate pipelines?
- What is your approach to hiring someone who is technically weaker but has high growth potential?

### Onboarding

- How do you onboard a new engineer to be productive within 30 days?
- Describe your ideal onboarding program for a senior engineer joining your team.
- How do you onboard a new engineer during a critical project delivery period?
- What metrics do you use to measure onboarding effectiveness?
- How do you assign a buddy or mentor to new hires? What expectations do you set?

### Org Design

- Your team has grown to 12 engineers. How do you decide when and how to split the team?
- How do you define team boundaries and ownership when two teams need to work on the same codebase?
- You are asked to lead a reorg that will move engineers between teams. How do you plan and execute this?
- How do you decide between a generalist team structure and a specialist team structure?
- Your company is growing from 3 engineering teams to 8. How do you design the org structure?
- How do you handle a situation where an engineer wants to transfer to another team?

### Team Culture

- How do you build a strong engineering culture on a new team?
- Describe the team norms you establish when forming a new team.
- How do you create an environment where engineers feel safe raising concerns?
- How do you handle a situation where your team culture is at odds with broader company culture?
- How do you celebrate wins and acknowledge contributions?
- How do you ensure knowledge sharing happens consistently across your team?

### Scaling Challenges

- How does your management style change as your team grows from 4 to 8 to 15 people?
- You are promoted to manage 3 teams. How do you adjust your approach?
- How do you maintain team cohesion when your team is distributed across time zones?
- How do you handle the transition from being a player-coach to a pure people leader?
- What processes do you put in place as a team scales that you would not need with a small team?


## What Interviewers Look For

- Structured hiring process. Clear interview loop design, rubric calibration, and debrief practices.
- Onboarding intentionality. Not just "they will figure it out" but a designed experience that accelerates productivity.
- Organizational thinking. Understanding of Conway's Law, team topology patterns, and how structure affects outcomes.
- Scaling awareness. How management practices evolve as team size grows.
