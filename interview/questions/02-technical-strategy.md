# Technical Strategy and Roadmaps

Engineering managers are expected to set technical direction without being the primary implementer. This round tests your ability to think strategically about technology decisions, manage trade-offs, and align technical investments with business priorities.


## Format

Typically 45-60 minutes. The interviewer explores your approach to long-term technical decisions, trade-off analysis, and strategic thinking. This is not a coding round - it is a leadership-level discussion about how you guide technical direction.


## Questions

### Architecture and Technical Decisions

- Your team owns a monolithic service that is becoming a bottleneck. The team wants to break it into microservices. How do you evaluate and plan this migration?
- How do you decide between building in-house versus buying a third-party solution? Walk me through your framework.
- Your team is debating between two architectures for a new system. The engineers are split. How do you drive a decision?
- A senior engineer proposes rewriting a core service from scratch. The current system works but is hard to maintain. How do you evaluate this proposal?
- How do you balance investing in platform reliability versus shipping new features? How do you communicate this trade-off to product partners?

### Technical Debt Management

- How do you quantify and prioritize technical debt? What framework do you use?
- Your team has accumulated significant tech debt over the past year due to aggressive shipping deadlines. How do you make the case to leadership for dedicated investment?
- How do you decide what percentage of sprint capacity to allocate to tech debt versus features?
- Give me an example of technical debt that became a business risk. How did you handle it?
- How do you prevent tech debt from accumulating in the first place?

### Roadmap Planning

- How do you build a technical roadmap that aligns with the product roadmap?
- Your VP asks you to cut 30% of your technical roadmap to accelerate feature delivery. How do you decide what to cut?
- How do you plan a multi-quarter migration while maintaining feature velocity?
- Describe how you prioritize infrastructure investments when there are multiple competing needs.
- How do you communicate technical roadmap decisions to non-technical stakeholders?

### Technology Adoption

- Your team wants to adopt a new framework/language. How do you evaluate whether to proceed?
- How do you manage the risk of adopting new technology in a production environment?
- An engineer is pushing for a technology choice based on personal preference rather than team needs. How do you handle this?
- How do you ensure your team stays current with industry trends without chasing every new tool?

### Operational Excellence

- How do you define and track SLAs/SLOs for your team's services?
- Your team's service had 3 major outages last quarter. What steps do you take to improve reliability?
- How do you design on-call rotations that balance coverage with engineer wellbeing?
- Walk me through your post-mortem process. How do you ensure action items are actually completed?
- How do you build a culture of operational excellence without creating excessive process?


## What Interviewers Look For

- Trade-off reasoning. Not "we should use microservices" but "here is why microservices make sense for this specific situation and here are the costs."
- Business awareness. Technical decisions connected to business impact - revenue, customer experience, team velocity.
- Collaborative decision-making. EMs who describe involving their team in technical decisions, not making unilateral calls.
- Long-term thinking. How you balance short-term delivery pressure with long-term technical health.
