# People Management Scenarios

The most critical interview category for engineering managers. This is where companies assess whether you can actually lead people - not just manage projects or review code.

Companies with dedicated people management rounds include Google, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, Stripe, and most top-tier companies. This round appears in 80%+ of EM interview loops.


## Format

Typically 45-60 minutes, scenario-based. The interviewer presents management situations and probes your approach, reasoning, and judgment. Some companies use role-play format where the interviewer plays the engineer and you respond in real-time.


## Questions

### Performance Management

- One of your engineers has been underperforming for 3 months. They used to be a strong contributor. What do you do?
- How do you write a performance review for an engineer who is meeting expectations but not growing?
- Walk me through how you would put someone on a performance improvement plan. What specific milestones would you set?
- An engineer on your team received feedback from peers that they are difficult to work with. The engineer disagrees. How do you handle this?
- You have to deliver a "does not meet expectations" rating to an engineer who thinks they are doing well. Walk me through the conversation.
- How do you calibrate performance across a team of 8 engineers at different levels?
- An engineer is technically strong but consistently misses deadlines. How do you address this?

### Coaching and Career Development

- A senior engineer on your team wants to become a tech lead. How do you help them get there?
- One of your best engineers tells you they are bored and considering leaving. What do you do?
- How do you design stretch assignments that accelerate growth without causing burnout?
- A mid-level engineer has been at the same level for 2 years and is frustrated about not being promoted. How do you handle the conversation?
- How do you coach an engineer who is technically strong but struggles with communication?
- Describe how you would build a career development plan for a new hire in their first 90 days.
- How do you identify high-potential engineers on your team?

### Conflict Resolution

- Two senior engineers on your team have a fundamental disagreement about architecture. It is affecting team dynamics. How do you resolve it?
- A tech lead on your team is making decisions without consulting the rest of the team. Engineers are frustrated. What do you do?
- An engineer comes to you saying they feel micromanaged by their tech lead. How do you handle it?
- You learn that an engineer on your team made a derogatory comment about a colleague. What steps do you take?
- Two of your reports are not communicating well and it is causing integration issues. How do you address this?

### Retention and Engagement

- How do you identify early warning signs that an engineer is about to leave?
- Your team just went through a difficult quarter - missed deadlines, production incidents, and team morale is low. How do you turn it around?
- An engineer on your team receives an outside offer with 30% higher compensation. They want to stay but need you to match it. What do you do?
- How do you maintain team engagement during a reorg when roles and responsibilities are changing?
- How do you build psychological safety on your team? Give me a specific example.

### 1:1 Practices

- How do you structure your 1:1s? What do you cover and how often?
- An engineer is not bringing anything to discuss in 1:1s. How do you make them productive?
- How do you balance discussing tactical work versus career development in 1:1s?
- An engineer shares personal problems in a 1:1 that are affecting their work. How do you handle it?


## What Interviewers Look For

- Specificity over philosophy. "I had a conversation with an engineer who was underperforming because of unclear expectations. I set specific milestones and checked in weekly" beats "I believe in regular feedback."
- Willingness to have hard conversations. Candidates who avoid discussing PIPs, terminations, or direct negative feedback signal weak management.
- Empathy balanced with accountability. Strong EMs care about their people and hold them to high standards.
- Scalable practices. How do your management practices work with 5 reports? 12 reports? Are they sustainable?
