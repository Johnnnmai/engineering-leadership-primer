# Behavioral and Leadership Questions

These are typically "tell me about a time you ..." questions focused on leadership, conflict, influence, and values.

Based on candidate reports from Reddit, X, and personal blogs about what they were actually asked in engineering management interviews.


## Format

Typically 30-60 minutes, conversational. The interviewer picks a theme (conflict, leadership, failure, team-building) and goes deep on one or two scenarios rather than asking many surface-level questions.

Common formats:

- Single-scenario deep dive: the interviewer picks one situation from your experience and spends most of the round exploring it from every angle
- Category-based rotation: the interviewer covers 3-4 topics (conflict, leadership, failure, motivation) with one question each


## Common Behavioral Questions

### Leadership and Ownership

- Tell me about a time you took ownership of a problem that was not technically your responsibility.
- Describe a situation where you had to lead your team through a significant change (reorg, pivot, layoffs).
- Tell me about a time you made an unpopular decision. How did you communicate it and handle the reaction?
- Describe a time you failed as a manager. What did you learn and what did you change?
- Tell me about a time you had to advocate for your team when leadership was pushing for something unreasonable.
- How do you lead when you disagree with a decision from your leadership?

### Cross-Functional Influence

- Tell me about a time you had to convince a product manager to change their priorities based on engineering constraints.
- Describe a situation where you had to align multiple stakeholders with competing priorities.
- Tell me about a time you partnered with design or data science to improve a product outcome.
- How do you handle a situation where your engineering priorities conflict with the product roadmap?
- Tell me about a time you had to say no to a stakeholder. How did you handle it?
- Describe a time you built consensus across teams for a technical decision that affected multiple groups.

### Conflict and Difficult Conversations

- Tell me about a time you had to mediate a conflict between two engineers on your team.
- Describe a situation where you gave an engineer feedback they did not want to hear. What happened?
- Tell me about a time you had to manage someone out of the organization. Walk me through the process.
- How do you handle a situation where an engineer disagrees with your decision?
- Tell me about a time you made a mistake that affected your team. How did you handle it?
- Describe a conflict with a peer manager. How did you resolve it?

### Hiring and Team Building

- Tell me about a time you built a team from scratch. What was your approach?
- Describe your most successful hire. What made them successful and how did you identify them?
- Tell me about a time you had to rapidly scale your team. What challenges did you face?
- How do you build a diverse team? Give me a specific example.
- Tell me about a time you had to rebuild team trust after a difficult period.

### Delivery and Execution

- Tell me about a project that was at risk of failing. What did you do?
- Describe a time you had to make a difficult scope trade-off. How did you decide what to cut?
- Tell me about a time you shipped something you were proud of. What made it successful?
- How do you handle a situation where your team consistently misses sprint commitments?
- Tell me about a time you had to recover a project that was behind schedule.

### Amazon Leadership Principles (for Amazon EM interviews)

- Customer Obsession: Tell me about a time you made a decision that prioritized customer impact over engineering convenience.
- Ownership: Describe a time you fixed something that was not your team's responsibility because it was the right thing to do.
- Hire and Develop the Best: Tell me about a time you coached an engineer through a career transition.
- Insist on the Highest Standards: How do you raise the bar on engineering quality?
- Think Big: Tell me about a time you proposed a solution that was more ambitious than what was asked for.
- Bias for Action: Describe a time you made a decision with incomplete information. What was the outcome?
- Earn Trust: Tell me about a time you had to rebuild trust with your team or stakeholders.
- Dive Deep: Tell me about a time you identified a systemic issue by digging into the details.
- Disagree and Commit: Describe a time you disagreed with a decision but committed to it anyway.

### Google Googliness (for Google EM interviews)

- How do you create a psychologically safe environment for your team?
- Tell me about a time you helped someone on your team through a difficult personal or professional situation.
- How do you balance being a supportive manager with holding people accountable?
- Describe your approach to giving recognition and celebrating team achievements.


## What Interviewers Look For

- Depth over breadth. One detailed story with specific actions and outcomes beats three vague stories.
- Self-awareness. Acknowledging mistakes, limitations, and areas of growth signals maturity.
- People-centered reasoning. Decisions framed around team impact, not just technical or business outcomes.
- Outcome specificity. "Attrition dropped from 40% to 8%" beats "the team was happier."
