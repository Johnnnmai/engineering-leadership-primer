## Description

The role of an Engineering Manager is often misunderstood as either "an engineer who goes to more meetings" or "a project manager with a technical title." This session cuts through theory to show the practical reality of the role.

## Topics Covered

- Defining the Engineering Manager from a practitioner perspective
- Core responsibilities: people leadership, delivery execution, and technical strategy
- Daily reality: 1:1s, stand-ups, design reviews, hiring loops, stakeholder syncs
- The balance between coaching, shipping, and strategic thinking
- Common misconceptions about what EMs actually do
- Essential skills for modern Engineering Managers
- Q&A session

## Key Takeaways

1. Define the EM's Role - understanding core responsibilities at the intersection of people, delivery, and technical strategy
2. Lead Through Others - how EMs work within cross-functional teams without being the primary technical contributor
3. Build Sustainable Teams - applying people leadership practices that drive both performance and retention


## Q&A During the Webinar

### How can a senior engineer transition to become an engineering manager?

For senior engineers, engineering management is primarily a people leadership role, not a senior IC role with extra meetings. Most "management" work is about growing engineers, hiring well, and shipping through others. Senior engineers already know how products are built and what is technically feasible. The flavor differs - writing code vs. growing people who write code - but the systems thinking transfers directly.

The specific skills to learn: how to run effective 1:1s, how to give direct feedback, how to manage performance, and how to hire well. After 3-6 months of focused learning on people management and delivery skills, a senior engineer should be ready to transition.

### How to narrow down what to focus on when preparing for EM interviews?

Focus on three areas: people management stories (hiring, coaching, performance), system design at the architecture level (not implementation detail), and behavioral/leadership scenarios. Most EM interviews weight people management heaviest - prepare 3-5 detailed stories with specific outcomes.
