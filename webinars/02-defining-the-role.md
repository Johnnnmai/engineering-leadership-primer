## Description

EM job titles are inconsistent. This webinar examines 980+ current job descriptions and industry research to clarify what companies actually hire for, helping professionals transition from individual contribution to leading engineering teams at scale.

## Topics Covered

- Data analysis: scraping and processing job descriptions using LLMs to identify patterns
- Role classification: distinguishing between people-first and delivery-oriented EM positions
- People leadership skills: hiring, coaching, performance management requirements
- Technical depth: architecture guidance, tech debt strategy, and operational excellence expectations
- Delivery focus: project execution versus team building as primary responsibilities
- Career strategy: actionable approaches for securing EM positions
- Industry trends: interview patterns and evolving skill expectations

## Key Findings

### Dataset

The analysis is based on approximately 980 job descriptions scraped from major job boards in January 2026. Jobs were collected from six cities: Bay Area, Los Angeles, New York, London, Amsterdam, and Berlin. After removing duplicates and filtering out unrelated roles, each description was processed using an LLM to extract structured information: company details, position type (people-first, delivery-first, or technical EM), responsibilities, management scenarios, and skills categorized by type.

### Skills

62% of the positions were people-first roles. The remaining 38% were delivery-focused roles or technical lead positions relabeled as "engineering manager."

People leadership is the number one skill pattern, mentioned in about 76% of positions. 88.7% of EM roles require skills beyond just technical leadership.

Top skills from job descriptions:
- Hiring and talent development is the dominant skill (76.3%)
- Performance management in second place (62.4%)
- System design and architecture guidance commonly expected (29.1%)
- Delivery management, cross-team coordination, and stakeholder communication are standard expectations
