# Learning Path for Engineering Managers

What to learn and in what order, based on skill demand from 980+ EM job descriptions and real hiring data across FAANG, growth-stage, and Series A companies.

For a structured deep-dive with 200+ real interview examples, check out [The 0→1 EM Interview Playbook](https://valenx.org/books/em).


## Core Skills

The most important 20% that accounts for 80% of the work. Based on hiring manager interviews, EM community surveys, and [job market research](../role/02-skills.md).

### People Leadership

- Hiring and interview loop design - the highest-leverage activity for any EM
- Performance management - reviews, calibration, PIPs, promotion advocacy
- Coaching and career development - growing engineers from junior to senior and beyond
- 1:1s and feedback - structured conversations that drive growth and surface issues early
- Conflict resolution - mediating disagreements, addressing toxic behavior, rebuilding trust

### Delivery Management

- Sprint planning and execution - agile ceremonies, capacity planning, velocity tracking
- Cross-team coordination - dependency management, shared infrastructure, release trains
- Risk management - early identification, mitigation plans, stakeholder communication
- Stakeholder management - status updates, executive reporting, scope negotiation
- Post-launch practices - retrospectives, metric tracking, continuous improvement

### Technical Strategy

- System design at the leadership level - architecture trade-offs, component boundaries, team ownership
- Technical debt management - categorization, prioritization, capacity allocation, tracking
- Operational excellence - SLAs/SLOs, on-call design, incident response, post-mortems
- Build vs buy decisions - evaluation frameworks, vendor management, migration planning
- Technology adoption - evaluation criteria, risk management, team enablement

### Cross-Functional Partnership

- Working with product - roadmap alignment, scope negotiation, priority discussions
- Working with design - user experience constraints, technical feasibility conversations
- Executive communication - crisp updates, decision memos, budget justifications
- Organizational awareness - navigating company politics, building influence, managing up


## Other Skills

Based on the [job market research](../role/02-skills.md). These are not covered in the core path above but frequently appear in job descriptions.

### Organizational Design

- Team structuring - charter definition, skill composition, team size optimization
- Reorg management - communication plans, knowledge transfer, relationship preservation
- Headcount planning - capacity modeling, business case building, budget alignment
- Conway's Law awareness - how team structure affects system architecture

### Engineering Culture

- Psychological safety - creating environments where people take risks and raise concerns
- Knowledge sharing - tech talks, documentation practices, learning time allocation
- Diversity and inclusion - hiring practices, team dynamics, inclusive decision-making
- Engineering standards - code review norms, testing requirements, quality gates

### Business and Strategy

- Budget management - infrastructure costs, headcount planning, ROI tracking
- Business impact framing - connecting engineering work to revenue, efficiency, and customer outcomes
- Market awareness - competitive landscape, technology trends, industry shifts
- Career development beyond your team - mentoring other managers, building PM programs

- [From Tech Lead](from-tech-lead.md) - natural progression, 2-4 months

## The Typical EM Tool Stack

- Project management - Jira, Linear, Asana, Notion
- Engineering operations - GitHub/GitLab, CI/CD platforms, feature flags
- Monitoring and observability - Datadog, New Relic, PagerDuty, Grafana
- Communication - Slack, Loom, Confluence, Google Docs
- Hiring - Greenhouse, Lever, LinkedIn Recruiter
- People management - Lattice, Culture Amp, 15Five
- Architecture - Miro, Excalidraw, Lucidchart


## Skills by Priority

### Must have

- Hiring and interview loop design
- Performance management and feedback
- Sprint planning and delivery execution
- 1:1 practices and coaching
- Stakeholder communication

### High value

- System design at the architecture level
- Technical debt management strategy
- Cross-functional partnership (product, design, data)
- Conflict resolution and difficult conversations
- Organizational design and team structuring

### Differentiators

- Deep domain expertise (infrastructure, ML, platform, mobile)
- Budget management and headcount planning
- Executive communication and organizational influence
- Diversity and inclusion leadership
- Engineering culture building at scale
