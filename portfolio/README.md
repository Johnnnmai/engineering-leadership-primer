# Portfolio Project Ideas

(draft)

Project ideas that demonstrate engineering management skills relevant to the job market. Each project covers patterns that appear frequently in EM job descriptions and interview loops.


## Team Health Dashboard

Build a metrics dashboard that tracks engineering team health indicators and identifies early warning signs of burnout, attrition risk, and delivery slowdowns.

Skills demonstrated:

- People leadership (what to measure and why - engagement signals, attrition predictors)
- Data-driven management (sprint velocity trends, code review turnaround, meeting load)
- Proactive intervention (automated alerts for health metric degradation)
- Stakeholder communication (executive summary with insight narration)
- Engineering culture (connecting team health to delivery outcomes)

Deliverable: Dashboard design with metric definitions, alert thresholds, and a 2-page write-up explaining your management philosophy behind each metric.

Complexity: intermediate. Shows management thinking backed by data.


## Incident Response Post-Mortem

Document a real or simulated production incident end-to-end. Cover the timeline, response, root cause analysis, and systemic improvements.

Skills demonstrated:

- Incident leadership (communication, coordination, decision-making under pressure)
- Root cause analysis (5-whys, contributing factors, timeline reconstruction)
- Process improvement (systemic changes that prevent recurrence)
- Blameless culture (post-mortem framing that drives learning, not punishment)
- Stakeholder communication (executive summary, customer communication plan)
- Measurement (tracking MTTR, recurrence rate, and action item completion)

Complexity: beginner to intermediate. Post-mortems are one of the clearest ways to demonstrate EM ownership and operational maturity.


## Hiring Pipeline Design

Design a complete interview process for hiring senior engineers at a specific company type (startup, growth-stage, or enterprise).

Skills demonstrated:

- Hiring strategy (role definition, sourcing approach, diversity pipeline)
- Interview loop design (round types, time allocation, rubric structure)
- Bias mitigation (structured interviews, calibration sessions, debrief frameworks)
- Candidate experience (communication cadence, feedback timeline, closing strategy)
- Measurement (time-to-hire, offer acceptance rate, new hire retention)

Complexity: intermediate. Shows the highest-leverage EM activity.


## Technical Roadmap Case Study

Write a technical roadmap for a team navigating a major platform migration, tech debt reduction initiative, or architecture evolution.

Skills demonstrated:

- Technical strategy (migration planning, risk assessment, sequencing)
- Prioritization (balancing features, debt reduction, and reliability investment)
- Cross-functional alignment (product partner negotiation, leadership buy-in)
- Team capacity planning (sprint allocation, parallel workstreams, dependency management)
- Communication (roadmap presentation, stakeholder updates, decision memos)

Complexity: advanced. Shows strategic technical leadership at the EM level.


## Org Design Proposal

Design a team structure for an engineering organization scaling from 3 teams to 8 teams. Include charter definitions, ownership boundaries, and hiring plans.

Skills demonstrated:

- Organizational design (team topologies, ownership models, Conway's Law awareness)
- Scaling strategy (when to split teams, how to maintain culture during growth)
- Headcount planning (capacity modeling, business case building, level mix)
- Communication planning (how to announce and execute the restructure)
- Risk management (attrition risk, knowledge transfer, delivery continuity)

Complexity: advanced. Shows organizational thinking at the Director level.


## Team Turnaround Case Study

Document a real or simulated team turnaround - taking a team from high attrition and low morale to a high-performing state.

Skills demonstrated:

- Assessment and diagnosis (identifying root causes of team dysfunction)
- People leadership (rebuilding trust, coaching through change, retaining key talent)
- Process improvement (fixing delivery predictability, introducing effective rituals)
- Measurement (tracking morale, attrition, velocity, and engagement before/after)
- Stakeholder management (communicating the turnaround plan and progress to leadership)

Complexity: intermediate to advanced. Team turnarounds are commonly asked about in EM interviews.


## Engineering Metrics Report

Build a quarterly engineering metrics report that communicates team performance, delivery outcomes, and areas for investment to executive leadership.

Skills demonstrated:

- Executive communication (concise, actionable insights for non-technical audience)
- Metrics selection (what to measure and what to skip - avoid vanity metrics)
- Data storytelling (connecting metrics to business outcomes)
- Trend analysis (quarter-over-quarter patterns, leading indicators)
- Action orientation (each insight paired with a recommendation)

Complexity: beginner to intermediate. Every EM needs to communicate team performance upward.


## Interview-Specific Portfolio Tips

From interviews and hiring manager observations:

- Specific stories beat frameworks. "I redesigned our interview loop and reduced time-to-hire from 45 days to 28 days" carries more weight than a generic interview design template.
- Every project should include outcomes. "I turned around a team with 35% attrition and reduced it to 8% in 6 months" is memorable. Generic process descriptions are not.
- Include your decision-making rationale. Hiring managers want to see your judgment, not just your actions.
- Published writing signals engagement. A blog post about your hiring philosophy or team-building approach demonstrates that you think deeply about management.
