## 🎯 The EM Role

What engineering managers actually do, based on 980+ job descriptions.

| Guide | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| [My vision of the role](role/01-my-vision.md) | How I see engineering management, EM vs Tech Lead vs Director vs VP, the people-to-delivery lifecycle |
| [Skills analysis](role/02-skills.md) | Top EM skills, leadership areas, technical depth expectations, tools and frameworks |
| [Responsibilities](role/03-responsibilities.md) | Patterns extracted from 3,600+ job responsibilities |
| [Use cases](role/04-use-cases.md) | Real management scenarios showing what companies expect EMs to handle |
| [Reality vs. job postings](role/05-reality-vs-postings.md) | What candidates experience vs. what's advertised |

88.7% of EM roles require skills beyond technical leadership. Hiring and team building appear in 76.3% of roles. Only 5.2% are pure technical lead roles rebranded as EM - the market wants people who grow teams and ship through others.

## 🔄 Career Transitions

5 paths into engineering management, each with timeline, what transfers, and what to learn.

| From | Timeline | Your Superpower |
|------|----------|----------------|
| [IC Engineer](learning-paths/from-ic-engineer.md) | 3-6 months | Technical depth, shipping experience, engineering credibility |
| [Tech Lead](learning-paths/from-tech-lead.md) | 2-4 months | Technical leadership, mentoring, architecture decisions |
| [Product Manager](learning-paths/from-product-manager.md) | 3-5 months | Stakeholder management, roadmap ownership, cross-functional leadership |
| [TPM](learning-paths/from-tpm.md) | 2-4 months | Program execution, cross-team coordination, delivery discipline |
| [Consultant](learning-paths/from-consultant.md) | 3-5 months | Structured thinking, client management, executive communication |
